Nucleus Bounty: Utah Innovation Connections Hub
Challenge Brief & Parameters
Problem Statement
Utah has a rapidly growing innovation ecosystem driven by research institutions such as the University of Utah, Brigham Young University, and Utah State University. These institutions are producing high-potential deep tech startups in areas like biotech, advanced materials, energy, and AI.
However, a critical bottleneck exists:
University tech commercialization fails most often not at the research stage but at the market-problem match. Early-stage and university spinout companies struggle to find experienced operators (C-suite leaders, commercialization experts, sales talent, and technical operators), while many qualified individuals lack visibility into meaningful startup opportunities. Beyond university commercialization, many general startups also face the same issue, with current job boards and networking sites providing insufficient matching opportunities. 
Core Challenge
Build an AI-powered platform that intelligently matches talent to startups.

Nucleus ‘connections hub’ on website (squarespace) that uses typeform to feed into our CRM (Affinity). 

Issues
No automatic matching ability
Very manual process
Hackathon Objective
Teams must design and prototype an AI-driven solution that:
Improves quality, speed, and precision of matches
Accounts for Utah-specific ecosystem dynamics
Goes beyond traditional job boards or LinkedIn-style matching
Required Features
Each team’s solution should include the following core components:
A. Dual-Sided User Profiles
Talent Profiles:
Skills (technical + functional)
Industry/domain expertise
Stage preference (idea, pre-seed, growth)
Availability (full-time, fractional, advisory, internship)
Risk tolerance
Mission/interest alignment
Startup Profiles:
Technology domain (core sector focus, etc.)
Origin (e.g., university lab, bootstrapped)
Stage of commercialization (TRL or Funding Stage
Immediate needs (CEO, CTO, biz dev, regulatory, etc.)
Funding status (grants/venture)

B. Nucleus Parameters
The platform must be compatible with our existing softwares
Squarespace (website)
Affinity (CRM)
C. User Experience (UX)
The platform should clearly demonstrate:
How a user signs up
How matches are generated
How results are explained (transparency matters)
Strong preference for:
Explainable AI (“Why was I matched?”)
Simple, intuitive interface
Trust-building elements
Optional High-Value Features
Teams that go further may include:
Talent upskilling recommendations (“You’re 80% fit—here’s how to close the gap”)
Ecosystem mapping (who knows who in Utah)
Data Guidelines
Teams may:
Use synthetic datasets
Simulate Utah startup/talent profiles
Incorporate public datasets (if available)
Teams should not rely on proprietary or sensitive personal data.
Deliverables
Each team must present:
A. Working Prototype or Demo
Web app, Figma prototype, or functional backend
B. AI Approach Explanation
What models/tools were used (LLMs, embeddings, etc.)
Why this approach improves matching vs traditional systems
C. Utah Context Integration
How your solution is uniquely tailored to Utah
D. Example Matches
Show at least 2–3 realistic match scenarios:
Executive → deep tech startup
Student → research spinout
Operator → scaling company
Judging Criteria
1. Match Quality & Intelligence (30%)
Does it feel meaningfully better than LinkedIn or job boards?
2. User Experience (40%)
Is it intuitive, clear, and trustworthy?
3. Integration (20%
Ability to seamlessly integrate into Nucleus workflows
4. Innovation & Creativity (10%)
Novel approaches, unexpected insights, bold thinking
